# What is "Bessel's correction"?

>In statistics, Bessel's correction, named after Friedrich Bessel, is the use of n âˆ’ 1 instead of n in the formula for the sample variance and sample standard deviation, where n is the number of observations in a sample. This corrects the bias in the estimation of the population variance, and some (but not all) of the bias in the estimation of the population standard deviation, but often increases the mean squared error in these estimations. <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bessel%27s_correction" target="_blank">wiki</a> 


There are two ways to explain why dividing by (n-1) yeilds a *better* estimate of the variance than dividing by n:

1. Under-estimation of $$ \sigma^2 $$
2. Degrees of freedom!
